Problematic Polygons

the 'paradox of boundaries'

For generations, anthropologists have made representations of indigenous territoires drawing on conventional cartographic 'polygons' to depict neat, continuous territoires.

Research has suggested that the 'polygon' may being asked to do too much work connecting principles of Indigenous land tenure with relational ontologies of territory.

Our work is an experiment in cartographic alternatives to the polygon for representing Indigenous territorial relations.

relational cartography

Some (few) representations of Indigenous territorial relations have eschewed the polygon in favour of other modes of representing Indigenous connections to place.

One model, arcs that radiate out from home communities to places cared for and about by Indigenous peoples provokes different senses of territory.

Our project draws on the powerful deck.gl scripting language dynamically represent these territorial relations in a web map. Click below to see the cartographic experiment.
